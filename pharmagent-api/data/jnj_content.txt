# Johnson & Johnson Corporate Overview & Mission
Johnson & Johnson (J&J) is a leading global healthcare company founded in 1886. Its global headquarters is located in New Brunswick, New Jersey. The company's core mission is to blend heart, science, and ingenuity to profoundly impact health for humanity. Following the spin-off of its consumer health business (Kenvue) in 2023, the company operates under a unified brand with a streamlined focus on two high-growth segments: Innovative Medicine and MedTech.

# Our Credo
Written in 1943 by Robert Wood Johnson, "Our Credo" is the moral compass and foundational values document of Johnson & Johnson. It outlines the company's responsibilities to its primary stakeholders in the following order:

1. **First Responsibility: Customers and Patients**
   We believe our first responsibility is to the patients, doctors and nurses, to mothers and fathers and all others who use our products and services. In meeting their needs everything we do must be of high quality. We must constantly strive to provide value, reduce our costs and maintain reasonable prices. Customers' orders must be serviced promptly and accurately. Our business partners must have an opportunity to make a fair profit.

2. **Second Responsibility: Employees**
   We are responsible to our employees who work with us throughout the world. We must provide an inclusive work environment where each person must be considered as an individual. We must respect their diversity and dignity and recognize their merit. They must have a sense of security, fulfillment and purpose in their jobs. Compensation must be fair and adequate and working conditions clean, orderly and safe. We must support the health and well-being of our employees and help them fulfill their family and other personal responsibilities. Employees must feel free to make suggestions and complaints.

3. **Third Responsibility: Communities & Environment**
   We are responsible to the communities in which we live and work and to the world community as well. We must help people be healthier by supporting better access and care in more places around the world. We must be good citizens—support good works and charities and bear our fair share of taxes. We must encourage civic improvements and better health and education. We must maintain in good order the property we are privileged to use, protecting the environment and natural resources.

4. **Fourth Responsibility: Stockholders**
   Our final responsibility is to our stockholders. Business must make a sound profit. We must experiment with new ideas. Research must be carried on, innovative programs developed and mistakes paid for. New equipment must be purchased, new facilities provided and new products launched. Reserves must be created to provide for adverse times. When we operate according to these principles, the stockholders should realize a fair return.

# J&J Innovative Medicine
J&J Innovative Medicine (formerly known as Janssen Pharmaceuticals) centers on rigorous, patient-centered science to treat, prevent, and cure complex diseases. It focuses on the research, development, and delivery of advanced prescription medicines.
Key Therapeutic Areas include:
- **Oncology**: Focused on developing treatments for various cancers, including leukemia, myeloma, and prostate cancer.
- **Immunology**: Creating therapies for autoimmune conditions, such as Crohn's disease, ulcerative colitis, and plaque psoriasis.
- **Neuroscience**: Researching solutions for complex neurological disorders, including schizophrenia, depression, and neurodegenerative illnesses.
- **Cardiopulmonary**: Addressing diseases like pulmonary arterial hypertension (PAH) to improve survival rates and patient quality of life.

# J&J MedTech
J&J MedTech innovates at the intersection of biology and technology to make medical procedures smarter, less invasive, and more personalized.
Key Focus Areas include:
- **Cardiovascular**: Providing advanced electrophysiology tools to treat heart rhythm disorders (arrhythmias), heart recovery pumps, and vascular access technologies.
- **Surgery**: Developing digital systems, robotics, and instruments for wound closure, stapling, and precision surgery.
- **Orthopaedics**: Focusing on joint replacement implants, spinal care, and trauma solutions across the continuum of care.
- **Vision**: Innovating in ophthalmic surgery systems and contact lenses to preserve and enhance eyesight globally.
